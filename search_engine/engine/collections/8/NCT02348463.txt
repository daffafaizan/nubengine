
    
      The study has started 2007 and will continue until 2014. Pregnant women that will come to the
      maternal health care units in gestational week 8-11 will be offered to be screened for
      bacterial vaginosis. A vaginal sample will be taken by the midwife or by the women herself.
      The vaginal sample is air-dried and sent to the gynecological. There it is investigated using
      Hay/Ison classification for the diagnosis of bacterial vaginosis.

      Women with bacterial vaginosis will be offered treatment with topical clindamycin treatment
      for 7 days if the women has passed the 12th gestational week. A control sample for the test
      of cure will be done after 10 week. If not cured of BV she will be offered a new treatment.

      All women will be followed until delivery.


    
      Thirty-two patients with hemiplegic shoulder subluxation due to acute stroke were divided
      into two groups: shoulder supported slings and forearm supported slings. Hemiplegia
      rehabilitation passive and active-assistive range of motion, stretching, and neurophysiologic
      exercises were performed for all patients per day for 8 weeks.Pain assessment was performed
      with Visual Analog Scale, sensorimotor evaluation was performed with Fugl Meyer Assessment of
      Motor Function,daily life activities were assessed with Barthel Index, and balance was
      evaluated with Berg Balance Scale.

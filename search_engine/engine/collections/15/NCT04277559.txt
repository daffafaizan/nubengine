
    
      Preoperative anxiety can be detected via structured and standardized screening by the
      State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI) I and II. 225 patients scheduled for inguinal hernia
      surgery for any reason in the age range of 18-70 will be included in our study. Preoperative
      STAI I and II score (anxiety questionnaire) will be applied to each patient. The patients
      will be divided into three groups as preoperative patient preferential music, classical music
      and not played. The patient favorite music track and singer without limiting the kind of
      music or classical music will be played through the headphones. The STAI will be repeated at
      postoperative, the Numeric Rating Scale (NRS) and patient satisfaction will be measured at
      postoperative.

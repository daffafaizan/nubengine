
    
      The purpose of this study is to improve the perception of the experience of chronic pain, as
      well as functionality and quality of life in hemophilic patients with chronic pain

      Secondary objectives are:

        -  to change the perception that the patient has of their pain, to improve the coping
           strategies and to increase the perception of self-efficacy of patients in pain
           management.

        -  to increase the resources that allow a better self-regulatory of emotional, cognitive
           and competential of the pain experience, reverting in the emotional state of patients,
           particularly in levels of anxiety and depression.

        -  to improve functional capacity and musculoskeletal status.

        -  to improve quality of life.

        -  to determine whether changes / improvements are maintained over time 3 months after
           finishing the program

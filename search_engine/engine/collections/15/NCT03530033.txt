
    
      According to the random number method, eligible patients were randomly divided into 2 groups.
      Group A was the conventional surgery group, and Group B was the lidocaine group. The
      operative time for the separation of the sternocleidomastoid muscle from the two groups of
      patients was analyzed, as well as the length of time for the search for the accessory nerve
      and the effect of intraoperative nerve monitoring on the EMG signal.

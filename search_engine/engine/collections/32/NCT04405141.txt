
    
      This retrospective cohort study included 186 female patients who admitted to LUTS.
      Demographic characteristics, medical history, calcium (Ca) and vitamin D including laboratory
      studies and uroflowmetry results as maximum urine flow rate (Qmax), average urine flow rate
      (Qav), and voided volume (V) were recorded. Patients were divided into two groups according
      to age (18-50 and ≥51) and vitamin D levels (<20 and ≥20).

      Laboratory parameters, uroflowmetry results, were compared between groups.

      The study was approved by the local ethics committee
      (approval#22/06/2018-09.29-54132726-000-14136). The study was conducted in accordance to the
      Helsinki Declaration and Good Clinical Practices Guidelines.

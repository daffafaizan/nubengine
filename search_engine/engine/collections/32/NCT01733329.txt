
    
      Patients and methods: 120 pregnant women with risk factors for uterine atony who underwent
      cesarean delivery were assigned randomly to either 400 mcg misoprostol (n=60) or placebo
      (n=60) placed in buccal space after umbilical cord clamping. The primary outcome variables
      were the need for additional uterotonic agents, estimated blood loss and uterine atony.

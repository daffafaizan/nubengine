
    
      Spinal pain is a well recognized condition associated with significant personal and community
      burdens. Recent studies estimated the prevalence between 6 and 22% in neck pain, from 4-72%
      in thoracic pain and from 1.0% to 58.1% in low back pain, which increases with age. During
      the last decades numerous researches have been conducted on stretching effects, evidencing an
      increased muscle control, flexibility and range of motion. The main purpose of this
      investigation is to examine the effects of diaphragm stretching in spine and thoracic
      mobility in healthy subjects in order to apply the results in a specifics pathologies in
      future studies.


    
      Hip fractures are a major public health concern due to increasing ageing of the population,
      rising costs and associated morbidity and mortality. The problem is more serious in
      developing countries, because of limited economic and technical resources available. The aim
      of the following study is to analyze the epidemiology and early treatment results of hip
      fracture patients treated in our unit.

      This study will be done at the Hospital Rafael Hernandez Regional Hospital, a third level
      facility located in the province of Chiriqui, Republic of Panama. It is a retrospective
      observational study.

      For this the study will use the hospital records, including imaging studies were reviewed,
      including the Singh Index, and all relevant data will be analyzed with SPSS 21 software.

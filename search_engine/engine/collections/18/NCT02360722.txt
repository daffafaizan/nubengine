        1. The effect of long-term Oral Nutritional Supplementation (ONS) on improving nutritional
           and health status in Chinese elderly.

        2. The impact of long-term ONS on health cost including both medical cost and social health
           care burdens.

        3. The impact of long-term ONS on life quality of the elderly. The study includes Screening
           Visit (Day -7~0), and Visit 1(Day 0) to Visit 7 (Day 168Â±3). Enrollment of anticipated
           666 subjects, Male or female Chinese ages >= 60 years, to get 400 evaluable subjects.

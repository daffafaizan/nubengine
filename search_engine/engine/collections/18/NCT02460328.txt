
    
      22q11.2 Deletion Syndrome is the most common for microdeletion syndrome. The incidence is
      about 1:4000 of live birth. Clinical features in this syndrome are vary which consist of
      conotruncal cardiac anomalies, developmental disabilities, palatal anomalies, speech delay,
      hypocalcemia, characteristic facial features and immunodeficiencies. The most common type of
      immunodeficiencies is T cell defect that associated with thymic hypoplasia. In the present
      time, the investigators don't know about the resolution of immune defect in this syndrome.

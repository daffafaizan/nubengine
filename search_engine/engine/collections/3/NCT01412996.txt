
    
      In a prospective study, two hundred fifty patients with symptomatic gall bladder stone were
      randomized to SALC or conventional laparoscopic cholecystectomy (CLC) (125 in each group).
      The primary endpoint of this study was to assess the total outcomes of quality of life using
      EuroQoL EQ-5D questionnaire. The secondary endpoints were postoperative pain, analgesia
      requirement and duration, operative time, perioperative complications, estimated blood loss,
      hospital stay, cosmoses outcomes and number of days required to return to normal activities.

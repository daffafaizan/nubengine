
    
      The purpose of this study is to explore the symptom distress, fatigue, depression,
      uncertainty, and quality of life in heart transplant recipients, and associated factors of
      quality of life (QOL). The eligible patients are (1)who undergone heart transplantation were
      recruited from the Department of Cardiac Surgery, Nation Taiwan University Hospital,(2)18
      year old or older. A set of questionnaire with the Transplant Symptom Frequency and Symptom
      Distress Scale, Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale (HADS) , Fatigue Symptom Inventory
      (FSI), Mishel's Uncertainty Scale, and 12-item Short-Form Health Survey (SF-12) were used to
      collected symptom distress, fatigue, depression, uncertainty, and QOL .


    
      There is no robust evidence or guideline for fasting prior to contrast-enhanced CT scan in
      patients. Substantial heterogeneity exists on fasting prior to contrast-enhanced CT
      worldwide. Through dedicated questionnaires, the investigators plan to prospectively assess
      the prevalence and the risk factors for nausea and vomiting after the exposure to the
      non-ionic contrast media agent in adult patients undergoing computed tomography examinations.

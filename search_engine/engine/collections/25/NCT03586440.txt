
    
      -  in forward head posture there is flexion in lower cervical vertebrae that causes tension
           on lower cervical nerve roots according to Harrison systemic review and these lower
           cervical nerve roots supply hand muscles

        -  also in rounded shoulder posture there is weakness in scapular stabilizers and
           retractors which affect proximal stability of the arm

        -  proximal stability is important for distal mobility

        -  so from this biomechanical point of veiw we conduct this study to see if forward head
           and rounded shoulder posture can affect hand grip strength in asymptomatic young adults.

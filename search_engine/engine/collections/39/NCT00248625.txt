
    
      The Pediatric Acute Liver Failure (PALF) Study Group to identify, characterize, and develop
      management strategies for infants, children and adolescents who present with acute liver
      failure. The PALF study group includes 20 sites (17 in the United States, 2 in the United
      Kingdom, and 1 in Canada). The primary objective of the Pediatric Acute Liver Failure (PALF)
      study is to collect, maintain, analyze, and report clinical, epidemiological, and outcome
      data in children with ALF, including information derived from biospecimens.

      Patients enrolled in the PALF study registry were able to enroll in the NAC study providing
      they met the additional required inclusion/exclusion criteria.


    
      OBJECTIVES:

        -  Obtain quality of life and psychosocial data from older patients who are undergoing
           treatment for cancer and from their family caregivers.

      OUTLINE: This is a pilot, cross-sectional study.

      Patients undergo a 45-minute interview in person or by phone to provide demographic data and
      to complete quality of life questionnaires, including Functional Assessment of Cancer Therapy
      (FACT), Spiritual tool, Quality and Satisfaction with Treatment (QUEST), and Profile of Mood
      State (POMS), at baseline and at 3 and 12 months. Caregivers undergo a 20- to 30-minute
      interview in person or by phone to provide demographic data and complete quality of life
      questionnaires, including the Caregiver Reaction Assessment (CRA), Quest, and POMS, at
      baseline and at 3 and 12 months. Caregivers complete the Quality of Death and Dying (QODD)
      questionnaire 2-3 months after the patient's death.


    
      Maxillary expansion was performed for adult female patients suffering from maxillary
      constriction with bilateral posterior cross-bite & dental arch discrepancy average 12 mm
      using fixed Quad-helix appliance, for about 8 months. The appliance was delivered and
      activated at the day of corticotomy and bone graft (demineralized freeze-dried bone
      allograft). The activation was made half-molar unit each side. The patient was scheduled each
      month to activate the appliance or reactivate if needed. Corticotomy was performed at area of
      maxillary 1st premolar, 2nd premolar and first molar. Cone beam computed tomography was
      performed at three stages.

      T1 (baseline) Before appliance activation and corticotomy. T2 At the end of expansion. (about
      8 months of expansion). T3 At the finishing stage of orthodontic treatment (average 2.5 years
      from start of treatment).

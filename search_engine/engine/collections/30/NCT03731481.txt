
    
      This study compares the effectiveness of two virtual Lifestyle Medicine programs across two
      settings: urban and rural. Program materials include nutritional education, methods to reduce
      personal stress, and incorporation of physical activity in daily routine. Bio-marker and
      subjective/self-reported survey measures will be collected at baseline, 5-weeks and
      six-months from baseline. Study seeks to measure variations in participant outcomes across
      two lifestyle interventions, participant engagement and level of adherence.


    
      The study is a collaboration between the International Clinic of Rehabilitation (ICR) in
      Ukraine and the Karolinska Institute (KI) in Sweden. The intervention and the assessments are
      performed at ICR. The primary outcome measure, evaluation of gross motor function with the
      test GMFM 66 is filmed and the recordings are transferred to KI together with all assessment
      data. KI are responsible for study research design and collects, analyzes and processes all
      data. It is a double blinded study. Only two persons will know to which group the subject
      belongs, the person performing the randomization process in Sweden and the doctor performing
      the spinal corrections.

      There are two aims of the study;

        1. To evaluate the effect of "Biomechanical Correction of the Spine" (BCSK) in children
           with Spastic Bilateral Cerebral Palsy.

        2. To evaluate the effect of "Intensive Neurophysiological Rehabilitation System" (INRS) in
           children with Spastic Bilateral Cerebral Palsy.

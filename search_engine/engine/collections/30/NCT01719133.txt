
    
      The study tested - all by skin tests - in the same group of 20 trial subjects, one positive
      control (histamine solution), one negative control (placebo saline) and five experimental
      peptides called T1, T2, T3, T4 and T5. Results of the skin prick tests were scored as
      negative (no reaction) or positive (wheal diameter > 4 mm with erythema)

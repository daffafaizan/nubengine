
    
      Esophageal strictures in children may develop as a secondary to a surgically repaired
      esophageal atresia (anastomatic strictures) or as a result of chemical injury after caustic
      ingestion. Emerging strictures are dilated with balloon catheter at regular intervals.

      The possibility of various complications depending on the level of the stenosis during the
      dilation of esophagus. During Esophageal dilation may be pressed into airways and/or vascular
      structure.


    
      PURPOSE: The purpose of this study is to investigate the effects of virtual reality upper
      extremity training through Pablo system in patients with chronic stroke.

      METHODS:Patients with mild to moderate motor deficits were recruited and randomly assigned to
      "VR plus standard rehabilitation group"(n=19), and "standard rehabilitation group" (n=19).
      After 12 training sessions (60 minutes a time, 2 times a week), the performance was assessed
      by a blinded assessor. The outcome measures included Fugl-Meyer Assessment-Upper Limb
      section(FMAUE), Box and block test(BBT), Dynanometer, active range of motion of shoulder and
      elbow,and Stroke Impact Scale.Modified Physical Activity Enjoyment Scale and adverse effect
      were recorded after each sessions.Collected data will be analyzed with sample T test by SPSS
      version 20.0, and alpha level was set at 0.05.

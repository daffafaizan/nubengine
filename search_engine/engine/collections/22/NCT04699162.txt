
    
      Retrospective, observational study in patients with diagnosis of acute lymphoblastic
      leukemia, according to the criteria of the World Health Organization, from July, 2016, to
      October, 2020. The patients were cared for in the hematology department of the Hospital
      General de Mexico and in the hematology department of the Hospital Regional de Alta
      Especialidad Ixtapaluca. Relapse was considered in those cases with more than 5% of blasts in
      bone marrow and as relapse to the central nervous system with the presence of blasts in
      cerebrospinal fluid.


    
      It is well known that there is a high incidence of significant anxiety in pediatric
      population perioperatively, with adverse side effects in terms of emergence delirium and
      maladaptive postoperative behaviors. Many different strategies have been designed in order to
      minimize these negative consequences.

      Virtual reality is a new and simple technology that can be used at young ages. The aim of
      this study is to measure anxiety levels at different times in children scheduled for
      ambulatory surgery compared to patients with standard care without virtual reality.

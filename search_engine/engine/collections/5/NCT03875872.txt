
    
      Objective: To compare the postoperative outcomes between surgeries anaesthetized with
      propofol and inhalational anaesthetics in the Acute Pain Service records of Queen Mary
      Hospital for years 2015-17

      Methods:

      Retrospective

      Data collection

        1. Demographic data;

        2. Type of surgery;

        3. Type of anaesthetic techniques;

        4. Postoperative NRS pain scores (Rest and Moving) for Postop Days 1, 2 & 3;

        5. Accumulated amount of patient controlled analgesia (PCA) used in Postop Days 1, 2 & 3;

        6. Postoperative incidence of nausea, vomitting, dizziness and pruritus.


    
      Mothers 'perception of breastmilk insufficiency are important conditions affecting babies'
      exclusively breastfeeding in the first six months.

      This study was made to determine the effectiveness of structured education and follow-up in
      the management of perceived breastmilk insufficiency among breastfeeding mothers. It was
      tested the hypotheses that there are no differences between mothers who have been provided
      with structured education and follow-up and those who have not received the education in
      terms of initiating breastfeeding in the first one hour, breastmilk insufficiency
      perceptions, the rate of exclusive feeding of breast milk to babies 0-2 months old, the
      baby's amount of milk intake and weight gain.

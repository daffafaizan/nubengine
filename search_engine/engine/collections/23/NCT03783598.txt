
    
      It was planned as a single-blind, randomized controlled study with 3-month follow-up in
      adults. A total of 67 subjects were randomly allocated to the intervention (n=33) or the
      control group (n=34). This program prepared with consider to meaningful activities for
      individual and overcome to barriers via problem solving. That was designed as 6 modules
      including evaluations, diabetes education and problem-solving therapy. Each module was
      implemented at least 1 session per week, about 60 minutes, and was arranged according to
      individual needs.

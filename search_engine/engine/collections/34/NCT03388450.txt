
    
      A hundred and ten critically ill septic patients were included in the study. Patients were
      divided into two groups fifty-five patients each. Group A received enteral nutrition
      supplemented with Omega-3 fatty acid and group B received enteral nutrition without Omega-3.
      Demographic data, sepsis characteristics, number of patients required invasive ventilation,
      ventilation days, ICU sequential organ failure assessment score (SOFA), organ failure
      free-days, haemodynamic failure free-days, ICU stay, ICU and hospital outcomes were recorded.

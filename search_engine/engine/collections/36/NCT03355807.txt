
    
      The purpose of this study is to investigate the effect of magnesium sulfate on pain
      management for pain after sleeve gastrectomy operation.

      Design: A prospective, randomized, controlled clinical study.

      Setting: University hospital.

      Participants: Eighty patients undergoing sleeve gastrectomy.

      Measurements and Main Results: Visual analog scale for pain score, sedation score, mean
      arterial pressure, heart rate, and valid and invalid analgesic demand will record. Serum
      magnesium levels will determined at preoperative evaluation, postanesthesia care unit
      admission and at 24 hours. Side effects will also record.

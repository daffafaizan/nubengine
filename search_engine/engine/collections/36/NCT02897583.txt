
    
      52 subjects will be enrolled in the trial and randomized in a 2:1 ratio to receive either YAG
      laser vitreolysis or sham laser for symptomatic Weiss ring due to posterior vitreous
      detachment. Subjects will follow up at one week, one month, three months, and six months
      after the procedure. Assessments will include a questionnaire regarding duration of floater
      symptoms prior to presentation, severity of floater symptoms, number of floaters, and
      activity most inconvenienced by presence of floaters; Medical, ocular history and
      demographics collected; ETDRS and Snellen visual acuity; Optos color photography; Heidelberg
      Spectralis Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) and infrared photo; B scan ultrasound of Weiss
      ring with caliper measurement of nearest distance between Weiss ring and retina, Weiss ring
      and posterior lens capsule (only in phakic eyes); Slit lamp and indirect ophthalmoscopy with
      scleral depression of study eye; Applanation tonometry; Visual Functioning Questionnaire-25
      (VFQ 25). Qualitative change in Optos photography will evaluated by a masked physician.


    
      In this double-blinded, clinically-controlled trial, 100 child will be randomized into four
      groups of 25 patients each; control group, intravenous ketamine group received 0.5mg iv.
      ketamine, and two local ketamine groups receiving local ketamine by nebulization in two
      different doses; 1 and 2mg/kg. The verbal rating pain scale, time to first postoperative
      analgesic request, total analgesic consumption during 1st 24 h postoperative, serum ketamine
      levels, intra operative blood loss, postoperative bleeding, and adverse effects were
      evaluated.

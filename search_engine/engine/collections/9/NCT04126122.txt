
    
      Preeclampsia is considered severe if one or more of the following:

        1. Blood pressure of 160mmHg systolic or high or 110mmHg diastolic or high on two occasions
           at least 6 hours a part while the patient on bed rest.

        2. Proteinuria of 0.3gm. or high in 24 hours urine specimen or +1 or great on two random
           urine samples collected at least 4 hours apart.

        3. Oliguria of less than 500ml in 24 hours.

        4. Cerebral or visual disturbance.

        5. Pulmonary edema or cyanosis.

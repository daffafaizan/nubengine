
    
      Health care workers have high prevalence of musculoskeletal pain. Pain in the back and neck
      and other musculoskeletal disorders are the leading cause for long-term sick leave.

      This study test the effect of "Tailored Physical Activity" on self-reported days on sick
      leave and parameters regarding pain, function and quality of life respectively on the body
      function and participation level of health care workers which have experienced
      musculoskeletal pain related to the back or upper body.

      A minimum of 44 health care workers in DK-SÃ¸nderborg Municipality will be randomized for
      "Tailored Physical Activity" or a reference group.

      Primary endpoint is 3 months and 12 months follow-up.

5041. studies on hypoxia.  iii. thedifferential response of thebone marrow to primary andsecondary hypoxiatwenty-five adult male guinea pigs wereplaced in a decompression chamber, at asimulated altitude of 14,000 feet for 5 days,then kept in ordinary air for 5 days,and finally returned to the decompressionchamber to be subjected to hypoxia asecond time, for periods ranging from 1to 5 days.  during this period of secondaryhypoxia quantitative studies were madeof the changes in the blood and bone marrow.in secondary as in primary hypoxia thereis a significant increase in the nucleatedred cells of the marrow.  in secondaryhypoxia, however, there is a very markedincrease in the marrow lymphocytes,whereas in primary hypoxia the lymphocytesfall.  the marrow reticulocytes remainat a fairly constant level despite markedfluctuations in erythropoiesis.

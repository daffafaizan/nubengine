
    
      This study is single-blind because it is not possible to blind the practitioner performing
      the injection.

      Subjects were randomly assigned to the erector spinae plane block group (group E) and the
      thoracic paravertebral block group (group P) by a random random number table, and the
      possibilities for belonging to any group were all the same and can not be artificially
      controlled by researchers.

      Before the procedure, a resident who does not know of this study records the patient's pain
      and painDETECT. and on follow-up four weeks visits, PGIC scale is checked.

      The erector spinae plane block or thoracic paravertebral block should be performed after
      receiving the informed consent of the patient.

      Because of the large difference between the skilled and unskilled patients, the procedure in
      this study is performed by only one skilled practitioner.

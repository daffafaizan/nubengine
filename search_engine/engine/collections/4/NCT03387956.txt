
    
      One of the gold standards for analgesia following cesarean delivery is intrathecal morphine,
      which is not devoid of complications namely postoperative nausea and vomiting. We evaluated
      the antiemetic effect of intravenous dexamethasone combined with intrathecal atropine after
      cesarean delivery under spinal bupivacaine plus morphine anesthesia.

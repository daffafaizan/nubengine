
    
      In this study, conducted over o 12 months period, arterial blood lactates levels were
      measured in ICU patients at admission (H0), every 6 hours during the first day and and H48.

      This trial included traumatic, medical and surgical patients requiring ICU stay for a
      predictive period > to 24 hours.

      Blood lactates were evaluated as a prognostic parameter in predicting ICU mortality and
      compared to classic prognostic scores such as: SOFA score, IGS II and APACHE II.

      The primary end point was ICU mortality.

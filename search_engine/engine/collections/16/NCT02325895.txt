
    
      Forty-five randomly assigned into one of three treatment groups: 1) 50g dried plum; 2) 100g
      dried plum; and 3) control (0g dried plum). All groups received 500mg calcium and 400 IU
      vitamin D as a daily supplement. Blood samples were collected at baseline, 3 and 6 months to
      assess biomarkers of bone turnover. Physical activity recall and 3-day food records were
      obtained at baseline, 3 and 6 months to examine physical activity and dietary confounders as
      potential covariates

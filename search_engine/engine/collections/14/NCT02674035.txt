
    
      Inclusion criteria were female gender; 25 to 50 years of age; history of at least one
      pregnancy; body mass index (BMI) between 18 and 30 kg/m2; desire to undergo abdominoplasty as
      a single procedure without receiving liposuction or other cosmetic surgeries; deformities of
      the skin and subcutaneous tissues in the abdominal region; and musculoaponeurotic defect.

      Non-inclusion criteria were smoking habit; abdominal wall scar (except for a Pfannenstiel
      scar related to a Cesarean section); abdominal wall hérnias; history of deep-vein thrombosis;
      chronic obstructive pulmonary disease; câncer; hypertension; diabetes or other chronic
      systemic diseases; and use of corticosteroids. Patients lost to follow-up and those who did
      not undergo ultrasound examination were excluded from the study.

      Primary outcome: Get a technique that provides a safe correction with lasting results and in
      every segment of time.

      Secondary clinical outcome: confirms the plication of a single layer as positive in women
      patients at two years of surgery compared to two planes.

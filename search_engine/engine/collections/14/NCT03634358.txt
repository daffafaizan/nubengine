
    
      A prospective, randomized study, comparing two different techniques for pediatric male
      circumcision: the bipolar diathermy scissors technique with that of a classic scalpel
      technique. Study included male infants from 40 days old up to 18 month old. They were
      reviewed regarding Operative time, bleeding, complications, postoperative pain and morbidity.
      Differences in the outcome were compared.

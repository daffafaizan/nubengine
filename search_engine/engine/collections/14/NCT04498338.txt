
    
      After Laminectomy, patients are suffering from many problems as pain, weakness. This study
      was conducted on two groups of patients who underwent lumbar Laminectomy as a surgical
      treatment for lumbar canal stenosis. The control group received TENS and strengthening
      exercises, while the study group received neural mobilization in addition to the program that
      was given to the control group. The outcome measures include pain intensity, nerve root
      compression and functional level which were measured pre-treatment and post-treatment through
      using visual analogue scale, H-reflex latency and Oswestry Disability Index (ODI)
      respectively.

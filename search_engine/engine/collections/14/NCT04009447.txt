
    
      The investigators propose to utilize a behavioral intervention to manipulate sleep quality in
      150 adults with hypertension and comorbid insomnia, who will receive a 6-week Cognitive
      Behavioral therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) intervention, which has been shown to markedly
      improve sleep quality and promote consolidated sleep in approximately 60% of those treated. A
      lowering of nighttime blood pressure is one of several proposed mechanisms to be examined.

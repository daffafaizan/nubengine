
    
      A malfunctioning ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt will typically cause a rise in intracranial
      pressure, which can be difficult to identify without costly imaging. Novel, state-of-the art
      machine learning techniques can be leveraged to develop an algorithm that noninvasively
      estimates intracranial pressure. The resulting algorithm will accurately determine the
      presence or absence of ventriculoperitoneal shunt malfunction in patients with hydrocephalus.

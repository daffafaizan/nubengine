
    
      PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:

      I. To evaluate the potential of thermal therapy to improve quality of life by reducing
      symptoms of depression in cancer survivors.

      SECONDARY OBJECTIVE:

      I. To evaluate the potential of thermal therapy to improve quality of life by reducing
      symptoms of sleep disruption in cancer survivors.

      EXPLORATORY OBJECTIVE:

      I. To evaluate the potential of thermal therapy to improve quality of life by reducing
      symptoms of fatigue, anxiety and other generally debilitating aspects of increased stress in
      cancer survivors.

      OUTLINE:

      Patients undergo thermal therapy over 2.5 hours.

      After completion of study treatment, patients are followed up at weeks 1 or 2, 3 or 4, and
      then monthly for months 2-4.

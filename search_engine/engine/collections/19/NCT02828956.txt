
    
      N=92 adolescents referred to an eating disorders clinic meeting criteria for anorexia nervosa
      (AN) gave written informed consent to participate in this study and completed the ANSOCQ, the
      Eating Disorder Inventory (EDI-2), the Eating Attitudes Test (EAT), the Body Image
      Questionnaire (BIQ), two questionnaires measuring Self-Related Cognitions (SRC) and the
      Coping Across Situations Questionnaire (CASQ). After a treatment period of nine months,
      clinical AN diagnosis and the body mass index (BMI) were re-assessed. Exploratory factor
      analysis and logistic regressions were performed.


    
      Several previous studies have shown that clinical contextual factors, such as patients
      preferences, play an important role in the patient-physical relationship, with resulting
      consequences on clinical outcomes.

      The interaction between the gender of both patient and physician is often impacted by
      existing stereotypes.

      Patient preference for specific physician gender has been investigated in scientific
      literature.

      However the are no studies that analyzed the role of gender in osteopathic medicine.

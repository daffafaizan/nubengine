
    
      Chronic pelvic pain is defined as non-menstrual or noncyclic pelvic pain with duration of at
      least 6 months. This pain interfere with habitual activities and requires clinical or
      surgical treatment. It is a complex interaction between the gastrointestinal, urinary,
      gynecologic, musculoskeletal, neurologic and endocrine systems influenced by psychological
      factors. Its prevalence range between 3 and 8% among women aged 15-73 years, ranging from 14
      to 24% among women of reproductive age. It is very important a multidimensional approach in
      order to stablish a more specific profile.

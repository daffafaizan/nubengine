
    
      Two catchment areas in Hamburg, Germany with similar population size and health care
      structures were assigned to offer 12-month ACT (& IC; Department of Psychiatry and
      Psychotherapy, University Medical Center Hamburg-Eppendorf) or SC (Asklepios Hospital,
      Department of Psychiatry and Psychotherapy) to 120 first-episode and negatively selected
      multiple-episode patients with SSD. Primary outcome was the time to Service Disengagement.
      Secondary outcomes comprised medication non-adherence, improvements of symptoms, functioning,
      quality of life, satisfaction with care from patients and relatives perspectives, and service
      use data.

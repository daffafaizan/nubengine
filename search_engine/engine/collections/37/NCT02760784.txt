
    
      30 patients suffering from acute Achilles tendon rupture was originally randomized to each
      arm, and followed up after 6 and 12 months. Of these a total of 56 patients was included in
      the final analysis, and invited to participate in this additional follow-up, 4-5 years after
      their original injury. Originally patients was monitored using: ATRS questionnaires, ATRA:
      Achilles Tendon Resting Angle, Heel Raise Work: number of lifts, lift height and total work,
      and tests of strength and stiffness in the ankle using a kinetic dynamometer.

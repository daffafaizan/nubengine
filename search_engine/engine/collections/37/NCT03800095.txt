
    
      Patients suffering from haematological disease present symptoms of discomfort and currently
      benefit from palliative care skills only for the management of their end-of-life. However, in
      medical oncology, more and more studies tend to demonstrate the benefit on the quality of
      life of an early collaboration between the two specialties.

      Investigator did the hypothesis that early integration of palliative care with conventional
      haematological care could decrease discomfort symptoms and add a real benefit on the
      patients' quality of life .

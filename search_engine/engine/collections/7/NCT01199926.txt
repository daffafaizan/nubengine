
    
      The study was designed to assess the effects of vitamin D supplementation during exercise
      training on body composition, muscle function, and glucose tolerance. It was a double-blind,
      randomized, placebo-controlled, clinical trial with participants randomized into either a
      4,000 IU/day vitamin D or placebo group and all participants completed 12 wks (3 d/wk) of
      exercise training.


    
      In intravenous drug abusers (IDUs), as a result of previous intravenous substance abuse, the
      predictable difficult cannulate exist and cause the delay administration of antidote therapy.
      Additionally, IDUs are also at increased risk of carrying blood borne infections that could
      be transmitted to healthcare workers through needle stick injuries. The investigators aimed
      to compare intranasal administration of naloxone with intravenous route.


    
      PRIMARY OBJECTIVES:

      I. Measure the impact on discharge and transition satisfaction of utilizing questions from
      the Consumer Assessment of Healthcare Providers and Systems (CHAPS) patient satisfaction
      survey.

      II. Measure the impact of the Tool to Engage Patients in Discharge (TEPID) on patient's
      Readiness for Hospital Discharge and Problems After Discharge Questionnaire-English (PADQ-E)
      results.

      III. Measure the impact on decreasing healthcare utilization measures: readmission rates
      within 30 days from hospital discharge, hospital length of stay of admission in hospital, and
      emergency department visits within 30 days of hospital discharge.

      OUTLINE:

      Patients complete the TEPID checklist of items during hospital stay.

      After completion of the study, patients are followed up for 35 days.

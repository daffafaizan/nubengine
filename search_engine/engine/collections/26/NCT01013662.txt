
    
      The investigators enrolled 69 consecutive patients with severe sepsis or septic shock and
      divided them in two different groups after randomization with sealed envelopes.

      One group called intensive glycemic control with glycemic levels been between 80 to 110 mg/dl
      with exogenous insulin.

      The other group called conventional glycemic control with glycemic levels been between 180
      and 220 mg/dl.

      Blood samples were collected at 0, 12, 24, 48 and 72 hours to measure HDL, LDL, Total
      cholesterol, free fatty acids and oxidized LDL.

      Clinical data as creatinine, P/F ratio, bilirubins, platelets, lactate, arterial blood
      gasometry were either monitored.

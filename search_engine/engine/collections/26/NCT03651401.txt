
    
      Eighty nine patients with shoulder RCL and 32 healthy controls were evaluated. Patients with
      subacromial impingement syndrome (SIS) (n:25) and partial rotator cuff tears (PRCT) (n:28),
      who met the inclusion criteria, and 23 healthy controls were included in the study.
      Participants were assessed for SME, pain (rest, activity, night), shoulder girdle and
      scapular muscle strengths and upper extremity function (FIT-HaNSA). In addition, Constant &
      Murley score, the Shoulder Pain and Disability Index (SPADI), and the Nottingham Health
      Profile (NHP) were administered.

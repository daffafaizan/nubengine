
    
      409 ketamine abusers were recruited in this study, with 285 ketamine abusers participated in
      a 6-hour brief cognitive-behavioral intervention and 124 ketamine abusers attended
      educational lectures on ketamine abuse. A brief cognitive-behavioral intervention was applied
      to teach ketamine abusers about stimulus control, refusal skills, communication skills,
      decisional balance, and infectious diseases prevention. Stage of Change and knowledge about
      ketamine were assessed before and after the intervention.

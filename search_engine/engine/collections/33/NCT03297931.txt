
    
      Hypothesis of the study: Inclusion of pulses as part of a snack will lower appetite and
      post-prandial glycemia (PPG) in comparison to commercial, commonly consumed non-pulse snacks.

      Overall Objective of the study: To investigate and compare the effects of pulse and non-pulse
      snacks served as chips and dip on appetite and PPG in healthy adults.

      Specific objectives of the study: To test the acute effects of different pulse snacks on: 1)
      PPG and appetite for three hours, and 2) food intake 1 hour following consumption of snacks.


    
      Materials/patients and methods. Fifty-two consecutively enrolled patients with persistent
      asthma were included in a cross-sectional proof of concept study. Inflammatory asthma
      phenotypes were recognized by inflammatory cell counts in induced sputum. Breath-prints were
      analyzed by discriminant analysis on principal component reduction, resulting in
      cross-validated accuracy values. Receiver Operating Characteristics (ROC) was calculated.

      Legal and ethical aspects The study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of
      Helsinki principles (18th Word Medical Assembly, 1964) and was approved by the Clinical
      Research Ethics Committee (approval number: IIBSP/10/122/1161) of our institution. The
      participants provided their written informed consent to participate in this study and
      personal identification data were anonymized.
